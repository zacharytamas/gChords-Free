How to Read a Chord Chart
What is a chord chart?
The ability to read a chord chart is a very important skill for every guitarist to know. A chord chart, as are generated by this app, is a graphical representation of how to form a chord on the guitar neck. This article will serve as a primer for how to read any chord chart you come across.
Frets and Strings
The horizontal lines on a chord chart represent the fret wire on the guitar neck, dividing the diagram into frets. Typically, the first fret from the top is the first fret of the guitar, the second being the second, and so on; however, on certain occasions where the chord shown is located farther down the neck a fret number will be shown beside the first fret of the diagram, and all positions shown should be played relative to that fret.
There are six vertical lines on a chord chart—each representing a string of the guitar. From left to right they represent the 6th, 5th, 4th, 3rd, 2nd, and 1st strings of the guitar.
Reading Notes
A chord chart tells you exactly what to do on each string to form a chord. If there is an X above the string on the chart you do not play that string. An O above a line means you play that string open—play the string without fretting a particular note. If a dot is shown on the string, pay attention to what fret the dot is shown in, and place a finger accordingly on the guitar.
Barring
A common technique in guitar chord forming is the use a "bar" with one of your fingers. On some chord charts (gChords charts included) a bar is shown by a long line across several strings of a fret—representing that you should press a finger down across all of those strings. For an example of a bar, see the F Major chord in the Chord Library.
Fingering
Many guitar chord charts, including gChords, show fingering suggestions along with the chord diagram. In these cases, on top of the dots on the chart, a number is placed, telling you which finger you should use to press that string. Keep in mind these are suggestions: feel free to use whichever fingers work best for you.
